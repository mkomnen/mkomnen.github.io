STATEMENT

My name is Mariam Darchiashvili, I am a Graphic Designer from Georgia, currently based in The Netherlands. With main interests in print-making, editorial design and curatorial work, I also specialise in art direction, concept development, visual identity and research design as well as creative writing. A graduate of The Royal Academy of Art (KABK) I am open for collaborative projects, freelance work and new interesting ideas, unexpected topics or alliances.

With a special interest in mythological and historical narratives, inspiration for my personal work is deeply rooted in the past and naive; antiquity, renaissance and philosophy. I firmly believe in the beneficial power of dark colours and in using them in an interesting and thoughtful way. Both these aspects at times allow me to make cryptic choices. Philosophy and literature often become topics in my research-based projects, resulting in a text-based design that allows for a typology of type experiments to happen throughout the process. Enigmatic, yet functioning as a catalog of statements, I want people to interpret my work, feel it, or get confused.

I will call my approach to design practice as cynical. In the world of clean, joyful and objective design, I believe in our practice having the potential for subjectivity, obscenity and enigma. Just like in bookbinding, you’ve got to scratch the surface so that glue goes deeper.
